# Threat Intelligence

Collecting, analyzing, and operationalizing information about adversaries, their tools, and their techniques to support detection, response, and risk decisions.

---

## Tools & Repositories

- [MISP](https://github.com/MISP/MISP) — Malware information sharing platform
- [OpenCTI](https://github.com/OpenCTI-Platform/opencti) — Open source threat intelligence platform
- [MITRE ATT&CK Navigator](https://github.com/mitre-attack/attack-navigator) — Map detections and coverage against ATT&CK
- [ThreatHunter-Playbook](https://github.com/OTRF/ThreatHunter-Playbook) — Hunt procedures mapped to ATT&CK
- [ThreatHunting-Keywords](https://github.com/mthcht/ThreatHunting-Keywords) — Hunting keywords by technique
- [HELK](https://github.com/Cyb3rWard0g/HELK) — Hunting ELK stack
- [yeti](https://github.com/yeti-platform/yeti) — Threat intelligence management platform
- [CyberChef](https://github.com/gchq/CyberChef) — Data transformation and IOC decoding
- [dnstwist](https://github.com/elceef/dnstwist) — Domain permutation engine for phishing detection
- [APTnotes](https://github.com/kbandla/APTnotes) — Public APT campaign documents

## Books & Learning

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|---|---|
| The Threat Intelligence Handbook | CyberEdge Group | Free PDF covering SOC-focused TI fundamentals |
| Intelligence-Driven Incident Response | Brown & Roberts | Applies the F3EAD cycle and Diamond Model to IR |
| Applied Incident Response | Steve Anson | Practical IR with Windows forensics and TI context |
